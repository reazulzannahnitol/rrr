Why Bulgaria? Key Benefits for Foreign Investors

Bulgaria is a prime destination for foreign investors, offering a strategic location within the European Union, competitive taxes, and a favorable business climate.

  • Attractive Corporate Tax Rate:
    Bulgaria boasts one of the lowest corporate tax rates in the EU at 10%, making it a tax-efficient base for your business.
  • Strategic Location in the EU:
    Bulgaria is ideally located at the crossroads of Europe, Asia, and the Middle East, offering unparalleled market access and logistics benefits.
  • Access to European Union Markets & Funding:
    Benefit from Bulgaria’s EU membership with access to grants, funding, and a large, free-trade market for your goods and services.
  • Competitive Labor Costs:
    Bulgaria’s highly skilled workforce and low labor costs provide businesses with a cost-effective operational base compared to Western Europe.
  • Fast-Growing Economy and IT Hub:
    Bulgaria is a technology and IT outsourcing hub, making it an ideal destination for tech startups and companies focused on digital services.
  • Double Tax Treaties:
    Bulgaria has double tax treaties with over 70 countries, allowing foreign companies to avoid double taxation on earnings.
  • Stable & Transparent Business Environment:
    With ongoing improvements in regulatory transparency and a stable political climate, Bulgaria is increasingly investor-friendly.

Bulgaria’s Taxation & Legal Environment for Businesses

Overview of Bulgarian Fiscality for Foreign-Owned Businesses

Corporate Income Tax (CIT)

Bulgaria applies a flat 10% corporate income tax rate, one of the lowest in the EU, attracting a wide range of international companies.

Value-Added Tax (VAT)

The standard VAT rate in Bulgaria is 20%, with reduced rates for certain goods and services. Foreign businesses need VAT registration if their turnover exceeds BGN 50,000 or if they engage in cross-border trade.

Dividend Tax

Dividends distributed to foreign shareholders are subject to a 5% withholding tax, which may be reduced or eliminated under Bulgaria’s double tax treaties.

Micro-Enterprise Tax Incentives

Small businesses, particularly startups, may benefit from simplified tax structures and compliance processes under Bulgaria’s micro-enterprise tax regulations.

Personal Income Tax

Bulgaria offers a low 10% flat tax rate on personal income, making it an attractive location for foreign employees and business owners.

Double Tax Treaties

Bulgaria’s extensive tax treaties with over 70 countries allow companies to avoid double taxation, reducing their international tax burdens.

Social Contributions & Labor Costs

Employers are required to make social security contributions of approximately 18-19% for employees, ensuring a relatively low cost for hiring local talent.

Legal Requirements and Compliance Considerations


Minimum Capital Requirements

An OOD (LLC) in Bulgaria requires a minimum share capital of BGN 2, while an AD (JSC) requires BGN 50,000.

Corporate Governance

Bulgaria mandates that companies have at least one director and one shareholder. Our experts help you set up a compliant corporate governance structure.

Labor Law Compliance

Companies in Bulgaria must follow local labor laws, covering employee contracts, minimum wage, and working conditions. We assist with legal compliance for employment contracts.

Data Protection (GDPR Compliance)

As an EU member state, Bulgaria requires businesses to comply with GDPR. We offer GDPR compliance guidance to ensure data protection standards are met.

Accounting & Financial Reporting

Bulgarian companies must maintain annual financial statements and submit them to the National Revenue Agency. Our team provides ongoing support for accounting and reporting.

FAQ

How long does it take to register a company in Bulgaria? ▼

A standard company registration takes approximately 5–10 business days, depending on document preparation and approval.

Can foreigners fully own a Bulgarian company? ▼

Yes, foreign nationals can own 100% of a Bulgarian company.

Do I need a local director to register a company in Bulgaria? ▼

No, foreigners can act as directors, but a local address is required for business registration.

What is the minimum capital requirement for an OOD (LLC) in Bulgaria? ▼

The minimum share capital for an OOD is BGN 2, and for an AD, it’s BGN 50,000.

Are there specific tax benefits for tech or R&D companies in Bulgaria? ▼

Yes, Bulgaria provides incentives for R&D and technology companies, including deductions and support programs.
